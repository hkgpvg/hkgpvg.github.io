I Saw a Spider Then Finished the Other Eye

by Hanyi

Below the mirror, a spider—
the water rising to meet its abdomen, no larger than my pinky nail, legs clumping. The spider clung slowly, an inch below the lip, the way I imagine someone might try a door, already knowing it is locked. It slips down, tries again.
My hand hovered at the faucet, close enough to act. The spider kept clinging and I kept drawing the same line across my eyelid, stopping, starting over, stopping again.
The water lifted the body slowly until the movement stopped.
I turned on the tap.
The spider circled the drain twice.
The mirror had begun to fog. My eyeliner was drying.
I turned off the tap, and finished the other eye.
