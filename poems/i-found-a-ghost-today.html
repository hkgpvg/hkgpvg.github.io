I Found a Ghost Today

by Hanyi

i.
pressed between the pages of grandma's cookbook, its body thinned to a crease in the paper.
I brushed dust from the recipe, but her handwriting came away too— drifting to the floor with everything I could no longer ask.
ii.
in the kitchen window, rain gathered where her reflection used to stand.
the kettle hissed. somebody had left a cup beside the sink.
for a moment, I thought absence had weight.
iii.
behind the house, the swing still leaned forward as though someone had just risen.
Pa's jacket hung by the door, one sleeve turned inside out.
I kept expecting a hand to reach through it.
iv.
later, I tried saying her name aloud.
the syllables loosened, unraveling across the floor like thread pulled from a seam.
neither of us knew how to gather them again.
v.
all day, I thought I was looking for a ghost.
but ghosts are easy.
harder are the things that stay:
perfume caught in a hallway, a cookbook swollen with steam, the shape a body leaves in an empty room.
maybe I never found her.
maybe she found me first.
