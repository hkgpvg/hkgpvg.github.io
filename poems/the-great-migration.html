The Great Migration

by Hanyi

1. Field Note
The herd reached the Mara River
before the moon rose.
At the bank, three calves were taken
by the current.
One was taken by a crocodile.
2. Crossing
We move through the river’s unfinished
sentence. It’s a great black lung, or the breath inside a prayer. I thought I heard you calling
from the opposite bank, your voice cracked with heat,
thinning across the water.
When I try to answer, the river loosens
the ground beneath me.
The river pulls at us without warning.
When the calves don’t make it across,
the ones who stay behind become the lucky ones.
When the shoreline is too thin
for panic, not even words can press
against a mother’s ribs the way a calf does.
Before this river deepened,
I watched you step forward, your hooves unsure,
every instinct turned
toward the opposite bank.
The herd ran like laughter, startling
the egrets into the air. We slipped on mud,
clumsy and foolish in our joy. You touched
my shoulder. It’s only water, you said.
You took one step, another.
Then the water rose.
The current did not give you back.
Where you died, my translation begins.
3. Aftermath
We reached the Mara River
before the moon rose.
We made it across—
or we did not.
